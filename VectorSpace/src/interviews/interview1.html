Interview One

What is your name?

Olivia

What is you highest level of education?

I completed a Bachelor's degree in Finance at Bellarmine University.

What did you study?

Finance with a minor in Accounting.

When will you / did you graduate?

I graduated May 2024.

What is your current job position?

I am a Compliance Testing Analyst at Republic Bank, so I I analyze internal processes, test compliance with regulatory standards, and report any findings or risk areas.

What tasks or responsibilities do you find yourself doing daily?

On a typical day, I review and test various compliance processes, monitor new regulations, and ensure that our internal policies align with the latest requirements. I document test results, identify compliance gaps, and often work with other departments to address any issues that come up. Communication is definitely a big part of my job, as I need to share compliance findings and ensure my team understands them.

What is your biggest work challenge?

One of my biggest challenges at work is managing multiple testing projects simultaneously, especially as regulations can change suddenly, requiring immediate updates to our processes. Balancing these projects requires strict time management, as well as the ability to prioritize tasks that carry the most potential risk to the bank.

Do you have a hard time starting work or continuing through a working session?

Starting a session can sometimes be challenging, especially when tackling large reports or documentation, which require a lot of focus. However, once I get into the work, I’m generally able to maintain momentum, especially if I break tasks down into smaller, manageable portions and use productivity tools to stay organized.

What kind of productivity apps, if any, do you use on a daily / weekly / monthly basis?

My team uses Microsoft apps, so we use Teams for communication and coordination, especially for quick check-ins. I use Excel for data analysis and managing large volumes of compliance data.

What features do you wish were present in current productivity apps?

I struggle with keeping up with deadlines for each project and not losing important messages and updates in long message chains, so those features would be very useful. Announcements and reminder features would be nice as well for important messages and such. Also, a more specific feature I could use would be an automated update system for any regulatory changes relevant to our banking.

How often do you think you would use tools like scheduling and tasks list?

I would use scheduling and task lists all the time. Scheduling helps me prioritize high-impact tasks, and task lists are essential for breaking down complex projects into actionable steps as well as not forget about important tasks.

How often do you find yourself receiving or sending announcements in a group setting?

Several times a week - I receive/send announcements related to regulatory changes, internal policy updates, and project status updates all the time. Communication is key in compliance, as everyone needs to be informed about changes to ensure policies are followed. However, doing this is harder without a feature in the apps we use.

Do you have any interest in custom developed modules that you or your team would want?

Yes, I'd really like the automated update system for regulatory changes I talked about.

Would you or your team make use of customizable dashboards / toolsets?

Yes, I'd find them really useful for making sure we can monitor key compliance indicators in real-time, like any flagged compliance issues or pending deadlines. We could also use these dashboards to manage team-specific goals and track progress toward monthly or quarterly compliance targets.

What features do you wish were part of our product that we have not mentioned previously?

Going off of the automated regulation tracker, I think a feature that could enhance the product would be a centralized information tracker that compiles important updates relevant to a team’s focus. Also, a live reporting tool with customizable templates would make it easier to compile and share information, helping us stay organized, report to higher-ups, and not waste time on long meetings.
