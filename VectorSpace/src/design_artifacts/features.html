Interview Two

What is your name?

Matt

What is you highest level of education?

I'm currently completing on my Bachelor's in Computer Science.

What do you study?

I major in computer science with a minor in math and Spanish.

When will you / did you graduate?

I will graduate May 2024.

What is your current job position?

I'm a student but I'm also a Software Development Engineer at UPS through a co-op. At UPS, I help develop a web-application for tug drivers (worker driving a vehicle used to move packages at the UPS airport).

What tasks or responsibilities do you find yourself doing daily?

I study for classes and do research for my thesis most of the time. I meet with my thesis advisor weekly to discuss my progress, findings, and issues I run into. I always keep a schedule of my assignments as well as try to plan out times to study in my busy schedule. Most of the time, I'm programming the software and test my work. Sometimes, I have meetings discussing my progress, bugs, and the future of the project.

What is your biggest work challenge?

Scheduling times to work on school work/projects is difficult because I'm very busy. Contacting my coworkers, especially my manager, when theyâ€™re busy and I need their help can be quite difficult especially when I'm working from home.

Do you have a hard time starting work or continuing through a working session?

Yes, I find it difficult to start work or stay on task as I get distracted easily.

What kind of productivity apps, if any, do you use on a daily / weekly / monthly basis?

I use Google Calendar to schedule my school and work days as well as other plans which is connected to my Outlook Calendar. I use Teams for quick messaging and meetings, and Outlook Email for emailing. I use Google Drive to store my assignments and to collaborate with class groups.

What features do you wish were present in current productivity apps?

I wish my current productivity apps had optimized scheduling or just some scheduling tool that made planning simpler. This tool would eliminate the time I spend trying to schedule/plan my days/weeks.

How often do you think you would use tools like scheduling and tasks list?

All the time, as I mentioned that scheduling is difficult for me. A tasks list would ensure that I don't forget about upcoming assignments and studying for quizzes/tests as well as work tasks including important emails and deadlines.

How often do you find yourself receiving or sending announcements in a group setting?

I find myself doing that very often as most of my projects are collaborative and require a constant stream of communication.

Do you have any interest in custom developed modules that you or your team would want?

A tool that would allow me to manage a series of code repositories would be helpful, similar to Github and AzureDevOps. Regarding my thesis, a tool for keeping track of my sources used would be nice to keep my set of sources organized.

Would you or your team make use of customizable dashboards / toolsets?

Yes, it would be nice to be able to see data on my projects on a homepage which would be something I'd customize my dashboard for. For example, a chart for if a database has failed or number of users would be nice.

What features do you wish were part of our product that we have not mentioned previously?

Optimized scheduling where the system would recommend study/free/etc. time for gaps in my schedule. Also, if I could give the system a list of tasks (such as assignment or work tasks) with importance rating and time needed to complete, and it would automatically recommend an optimal schedule to complete these tasks. Announcements where the announce can check if they read an announcement and the announcer gets feedback to make sure their team got the important message/info.
